# Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India today. It involves tricking users into transferring money through deceptive methods. 

A typical scenario begins when a victim receives a call, message, or QR code from a fraudster pretending to be a bank official, seller, or customer. The fraudster creates urgency or offers an attractive deal, convincing the victim to scan a QR code or approve a payment request. Many users mistakenly believe that scanning a QR code will help them receive money, but in reality, it initiates a payment.

In some cases, fraudsters send fake links that resemble official banking websites, asking users to enter sensitive details like UPI PIN, OTP, or card information. Once the victim shares these details, the fraudster gains access to their account and transfers money instantly.

Students and young users are often targeted because they frequently use digital payments and may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes misuse of personal information.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and caution while using digital payment platforms.
